Interdisciplinary Fine Arts

http://www.stolaf.edu/depts/fine-arts/

Director, 2002-03: Susan Bauer (Interdisciplinary Fine Arts), movement analysis, dance

Faculty, 2002-03: Dan Dressen (Music), voice, diction; Steven Reiser (Theatre), theatrical design and production; Matt Rohn (Art and Art History), art history

The fine arts major is designed for the student who wants a well-rounded program in the arts. One of the distinctive features of this interdisciplinary major is the integration of the disciplines of art, dance, theater and music in a way that facilitates a dialogue and collaboration among the art forms. This dialogue is enhanced by creating a balance between learning about all four art forms, grounding oneself in one or two of the art forms and broadening the dialogue to include other disciplines. The interdisciplinary work prepares students to develop an aesthetic perspective, to form criteria for arts evaluation and to reflect critically on the role of the arts in their personal lives, in society and in other cultures. This approach informs a wide range of career options, especially for those interested in working professionally as artists, administrators, teachers, writers or therapists. Students are encouraged to engage in some co-curricular arts organization, volunteer work in the arts, internship or special exhibit/performance event and also to consider off-campus study programs such as HECUA City Arts, Twin Cities; ACM Urban Arts, Chicago, ACM Arts in London and Florence.

REQUIREMENTS FOR THE MAJOR

Students arrange the major by developing individualized contracts tailored to their particular interests and areas of emphasis. These contracts developed with the faculty of the Interdisciplinary Fine Arts program should include courses that represent a complete, coherent program of study consistent with the goals of the individual student. Contracts should include evidence of theoretical and experiential components of four art forms and grounding in one or more of the art forms. Contracts should be developed no later than the end of the sophomore year but may be altered by mutual consent at any time after their approval. The student and the interdisciplinary fine arts faculty will periodically review contracts.

A contract includes the three interdisciplinary core courses: 174, 274 and 374. Each major must select an area of emphasis from the four fine arts disciplines: art, dance, music and theater. Interdisciplinary fine arts majors must complete four courses in the area of emphasis (see Four-Course Area of Emphasis Recommendations below). They also must complete two courses from a secondary fine arts area (see Two-Course Recommendations below). The student must take 1.5 credits from the final two fine arts disciplines (see One and One Half-Course Recommendations below). Students must complete an integrative folder with the assistance of the Center for Experiential Learning. The integrative folder consists of personal goal statements, portfolio of documented art or performance projects and papers and reflections on interests, abilities and values.

General Education requirements and electives are selected to broaden and yet complement the student's particular interests. In addition to course work, each interdisciplinary fine arts major should engage in some co-curricular arts organization, volunteer work in the arts, internship, independent study/research or special exhibit/performance event.

SPECIAL PROGRAMS

Students may arrange, as part of their contract, courses in an off-campus study program -- Interims: Theater in London, Manhattan Interim in New York City and Italian Art in Context. Semester/Year Long Programs: HECUA City Arts program in the Twin Cities; ACM Urban Arts program in Chicago; ACM Arts in London and Florence; St. Olaf College's Term in Asia, Middle East or Global Semester.

RECOMMENDED COURSES

Three Core Courses:

Interdisciplinary Fine Arts 174, 274 and 374.

Four-Course Area of Emphasis Recommendations:

Art: If the student selects art as the area of emphasis, he/she must take one full credit of two-dimensional studio art classes (.5 credits x 2) as selected from the following: Art 111, 112, 113 or 115. The student must also take one full credit of three-dimensional studio art classes (.5 credits x 2) as selected from the following: Art 114, 116 or 119. The student also must take two full credits of art history classes as selected from the following: Art 151, 250, 251, 252 or 253.

Dance: If the student selects dance as the area of emphasis, he/she must take one full credit of technique classes as selected with the advice from their interdisciplinary fine arts faculty advisor. The student must also take three full credits of theory classes as selected from the following: Dance 120, 124, 231, 232, 246, 260 or 264.

Music: If the student selects music as the area of emphasis, he/she must take one full credit of performance studies (.25 credits x 4, all the same instrument/voice). The student must also take two full credits of theory/ear training: Music 111/113 and 112/114. The student also must take one full credit of a survey course, Music 230.

Theater: If the student selects theater as the area of emphasis, he/she must take one full credit of introduction course, Theatre 110. The student must also take three full credits selected from the following depending on area of interest: Theatre 130 or 131, 232, 150 or 250, 270 or 271, 379.

Two-Course Recommendations:

Art: If the major chooses art as the remaining fine arts discipline from which to select two full credits applicable to the interdisciplinary fine arts major, the student must take .50 credit of two-dimensional studio art classes as selected from the following: Art 110, 111, 113 or 115. The student must also take .50 credits of three-dimensional studio art classes as selected from the following: Art 114, 116 or 119. The student also must take one full credit of art history classes as selected from the following: Art 151, 250, 251, 252 or 253.

Dance: If the major chooses dance as the remaining fine arts discipline from which to select two full credits applicable to the interdisciplinary fine arts major, the student must take one full credit of technique classes as selected with the advice from their interdisciplinary fine arts faculty advisor. The student must also take one full credit selected from the following depending on area of interest: Dance 120, 124, 231, 232, 246, 260 or 264.

Music: If the major chooses music as the remaining fine arts discipline from which to select two full credits applicable to the interdisciplinary fine arts major, the student must take one full credit of performance studies (.25x4, all the same instrument/voice). The student must also take one full credit of a course in music fundamentals: Music 130 (offered interim only).

Theater: If the major chooses theater as the remaining fine arts discipline from which to select two full credits applicable to the interdisciplinary fine arts major, the student must take one full credit of introduction course: Theatre 110. The student must also take one full credit selected from the following depending on area of interest: Theatre 130 or 131, 232, 150 or 250, 270 or 271.

The interdisciplinary fine arts major is required to take One and One-Half-Course Recommendations from the remaining two fine arts disciplines.

One and One Half-Course Recommendations:

Art: If art is used as one of the fine arts disciplines from which the major will be taking one and one-half credits, the student must take .50 credit of studio art classes as selected from the following: Art 110, 111, 113, 115, 114, 116 or 119. The student must also take one full credit of art history classes as selected from the following: Art 151, 250, 251, 252 or 253.

Dance: If dance is used as one of the fine arts disciplines from which the major will be taking one and one-half credits, the student must take 1/2 credit of technique classes as selected with the advice from their Interdisciplinary Fine Arts faculty advisor. The student must also take one full credit of a theory class to be selected from the following depending on area of interest: Dance 120, 124, 231, 232, 246, 260 or 264.

Music: If music is used as one of the fine arts disciplines from which the major will be taking one and one-half credits, the student must take 1/2 credit of performance studies (.25 credits x2 the same instrument/voice). The student must also take one full credit of a course in music fundamentals: Music 130 (offered interim only).

Theater: If theater is used as one of the fine arts disciplines from which the major will be taking one and one-half credits, the student must 1/2 credit of Theatre practicum (.25 credits x 2): Theatre 233 or 253 (both may be repeated). The student must also take one full credit of introduction course: Theatre 110

DISTINCTION

Interdisciplinary fine arts majors with a grade point average of 3.5 or above in the major and 3.25 overall may propose a project and apply for departmental distinction with approval of the interdisciplinary fine arts team. The chair of the major's area of emphasis must agree to the project. The proposal and project must be submitted and approved by October 1 of the senior year. One of the team members of the Interdisciplinary Fine Arts Program will serve as project advisor.

Senior majors wishing distinction in the major are required to do a special project that demonstrates learning in their area of emphasis and the ability to integrate the four art forms. Projects are to follow one of the following tracks: (1) The student presents or exhibits original works. A written paper of process and reflection is to accompany the event. (2) The student performs established work. A written paper of process and reflection is to accompany the event. (3) The student writes a major paper.

All projects go through an oral defense of the interdisciplinary fine arts team and a faculty member of the major's area of emphasis department. The granting of departmental distinction is based on the special project and the student's overall work within the interdisciplinary major and the student's area of emphasis.

COURSES

174 Exploring the Arts
This is a team-taught course in which students attend a variety of music, theater and dance performances and art galleries, on and off-campus, to explore the process of perceiving and describing the arts. This material provides the foundation for an exploration of aesthetic perspectives and the role of the arts in their personal lives, society and other cultures. Performance/exhibit fee required. GE: ALS-A, ORC. Counts toward the major: Interdisciplinary Fine Arts.

274 Perspectives on the Arts
This is a team-taught course in which students build on the process of perception and description encountered in Interdisciplinary Fine Arts 174, Exploring the Arts, examine interpretation and evaluation and begin the synthesis process, a major focus of the Senior Studies course. Students attend a variety of music, theater, dance and art events. Performance/exhibit fee required. Counts toward the major: Interdisciplinary Fine Arts.

374 Senior Studies
A culmination of the development of critical/analytical skills that facilitate and encourage the integrative focus of the major, this team-taught seminar is structured around a chosen theme, Critical Issues in the Arts in the Early 21st Century. Students select a creative research topic that explores the seminar theme from a specific perspective and integrates the four art forms. Performance/exhibit fee required. Counts toward the major: Interdisciplinary Fine Arts.
