Computer Science

During academic year 2002–03, the college created a major in computer science. The new major builds on existing courses and proposes the introduction of several new computer science courses that await formal approval. Pilot versions of some proposed new courses will be offered during 2003–04 as Computer Science 378 topics. Please see the program's web site at www.stolaf.edu/depts/cs/ for descriptions of proposed courses and further details.

The computer science major is available to continuing and future students. The computer science concentration remains available to students who entered St. Olaf during academic year 2002–03 or earlier. overview of the major
Computer science is the academic discipline that focuses on concepts of computing and develops valuable analytical skills in theory, abstraction and design. St. Olaf's computer science program employs "hands-on" experience to reinforce abstract concepts. The program's primary objective is to offer a strong presentation of the concepts and practices of computer science. The courses below represent national expectations for an undergraduate computer science curriculum. The program also incorporates a distinct liberal arts perspective, including emphasis on teamwork and communication skills, examination of ethical and social issues in computing, and collaboration in upper-level interdisciplinary projects.

A lthough computer science differs from programming, information technology, information systems, etc., the study of computer science serves as excellent preparation for careers in such fields, because the concepts of computer science provide insights into all forms of computing. The program's "hands-on" teaching approach develops valuable technical skills, and the emphases on interpersonal skills and ethical analysis enhance any career, whether one becomes an occasional computer user or a computing
professional.

Requirements of the major

  • Either Principles of Computer Science (existing CS 172) and Software Design and Implementation (existing CS 272)or Accelerated Principles of Computer Science (new course);
  • Mathematical Foundations of Computing (new);
  • Hardware Design (renamed existing CS 274);
  • Algorithms and Data Structures (renamed existing CS 372);
  • Ethical and Social Issues in Computing (new); either Theory of Computation (new) or Programming Languages (existing CS 376);
  • either Operating Systems (new, formerly a regular CS 378 topic) or Client-Server Applications (new);
  • Senior Team Project (new); and
  • two additional electives.

These requirements represent four levels:

  • A breadth-first introduction to the discipline, through a “hands-on” survey of concepts (Principles of Computer Science) and three “second courses” that may be taken in any order (note that these offer non-majors a variety of one- to four-course samples of computer science);
  • Four “core” courses representing studies of algorithms and data structures, ethics of computing, computer languages and computer systems;
  • A deeper exposure to selected aspects of the discipline through electives; and
  • A senior-level “capstone” integrative experience (senior team project).
